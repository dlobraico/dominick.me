About

I'm a fourth year at the University of Chicago expecting Bachelor of Arts degrees in Computer Science and Public Policy in June of 2013. After graduation I'll be moving back to the east coast to work in New York City.

I've been wanting a place to call my online home since I shutdown my middle school blog but just haven't had the time to get around to building something satisfactory. This is a first attempt at filling that void. I'm not sure what I'll post here but I think more often than not it will be links with short descriptions or brief ramblings about whatever I find interesting enough to mention.

This site serves a second purpose in that I have been using my time spent working on it to try out some new web development options. An initial prototype was written in Clojure with the excellent Noir web framework. The current iteration is a custom-written and standalone OCaml application that uses the cohttp to implement a simple web server and COW to implement some simplistic templating.

You can reach me by emailing the address composed of my first initial at lobraico.com.
